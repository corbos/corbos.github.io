Kablamo! created by David Smelser

Blue's Turn

Rules

  • There are two players: Red and Blue.
  • Players take turns. Blue starts.
  • Each player has three shades of their color.
  • The darkest, the initial corner, is the player's home. The object of the game is to destroy your opponent's home.
  • Medium shade squares are active. A player selects one active square on their turn. (More below.)
  • Light squares are inactive. They become active on a player's turn, after an active square is selected.
  • When a player selects an active square, it "explodes". Effects:
    • The active square becomes empty.
    • A ray of inactive squares shoots up, down, left, right, and in the four diagonal directions.
    • Inactive square-rays stop when they hit an occupied square.
    • If the occupied square is a barrier or the player's color, the ray stops.
    • If the occupied square belongs to the opponent, the opponent's square is destroyed and becomes empty.
  • When a player hits their opponent's home square. They win.
